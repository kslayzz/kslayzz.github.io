# Virtual CFO vs. Full-Time CFO: What's Right for Your Stage?

One of the most common questions founders ask us — usually around Series A or when they first start dealing with institutional investors — is this: *Do we need a full-time CFO, or is a Virtual CFO enough?*

The honest answer is: it depends on your stage, your complexity, and what you actually need a CFO to do. This article lays out the framework we use when advising founder-led companies on this decision.

## What a CFO Actually Does

Before comparing models, it helps to be precise about the CFO function. In a mature company, a CFO wears several hats:

1. **Controller function** — ensuring books are accurate, taxes are filed, compliance is maintained
2. **Reporting function** — MIS, board packs, investor reporting, cash flow monitoring
3. **Strategic function** — business model analysis, pricing, capital allocation, unit economics
4. **Fundraising function** — data room preparation, due diligence management, banker relationships
5. **Stakeholder function** — managing banks, auditors, investors, regulatory bodies

A Virtual CFO typically handles functions 1 through 4 on an ongoing basis, often supplemented by your accounting team. A full-time CFO does all five — and in a company where the fifth function is full-time work (say, a pre-IPO company), there is no substitute.

## The Virtual CFO Model

A Virtual CFO (vCFO) is typically a qualified CA or finance professional — or a firm like KMA — engaged on a retainer or defined-scope basis. You get senior finance leadership without a senior finance salary.

### What it typically covers:
- Monthly/quarterly MIS preparation and analysis
- Cash flow forecasting and working capital management
- Investor reporting and board deck financials
- Tax planning and compliance oversight
- Fundraise readiness — structuring data rooms, reviewing term sheets, advising on cap tables
- Banking and lender relationship management
- Ad hoc strategic finance analysis

### What it typically doesn't cover:
- Day-to-day accounts team management (you still need an in-house accountant)
- Real-time decision-making in crisis situations
- Attending all investor meetings in person (though many vCFOs do attend key ones)
- Internal hiring and building a finance function

### Typical cost:
A quality Virtual CFO engagement for a Series A–stage company in India costs between ₹50,000 and ₹2,00,000 per month depending on scope — compared to a full-time CFO hire at ₹40–80 lakh per year in total cost.

## The Full-Time CFO

A full-time CFO is a senior executive — often equity-compensated — who is embedded in the leadership team. This person brings not just skills but relationships, institutional memory, and the ability to move fast.

### When a full-time CFO is clearly necessary:
- You are preparing for an IPO or large institutional raise
- You have a complex, multi-entity structure across jurisdictions
- Your business has significant treasury, hedging, or financial products exposure
- The CFO function requires constant external representation (banks, investors, regulators)
- You are past Series B and your finance function manages a team of 5+ people

### The honest challenge of hiring a full-time CFO too early:
- The right CFO for a ₹10 crore ARR company is different from the right one at ₹100 crore ARR
- A strong full-time hire at Series A often gets bored or is overqualified for the work at hand
- Equity dilution and cash costs are significant when the function doesn't yet justify them

## A Framework for Your Decision

Use this as a rough guide:

| Stage | Revenue / ARR | Recommended Model |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| Pre-seed / Seed | < ₹5 Cr | Founder-managed + CA firm for compliance |
| Series A | ₹5–50 Cr | Virtual CFO (strong retainer) |
| Series B | ₹50–200 Cr | Virtual CFO transitioning to full-time or full-time hire |
| Series C+ / Pre-IPO | > ₹200 Cr | Full-time CFO essential |

These are rough thresholds — investor expectations, business complexity, and the founder's own financial comfort all shift the curve.

## What Investors Actually Want

A question we hear often: *Will our investors insist on a full-time CFO?*

Most institutional Series A investors — particularly Indian VCs — are comfortable with a strong Virtual CFO arrangement at early stages, provided:

1. The books are clean and audit-ready
2. MIS is produced on time and is accurate
3. The founder can speak intelligently about unit economics and cash runway
4. Due diligence requests are handled promptly

Where investors push for a full-time CFO is typically at Series B and beyond, particularly when they are planning for the next round or eventual liquidity event. If your lead investor in a large Series B round is a global PE fund, they will almost certainly want a CFO with CFO experience on the cap table.

## Hybrid Approaches

The most common arrangement we see at Series A–stage companies is a hybrid: a **Virtual CFO firm handling strategic and reporting functions**, paired with a **senior finance manager or Financial Controller in-house** handling day-to-day accounts, payroll, and compliance execution.

This gives you:
- Senior strategic oversight without the senior executive cost
- In-house continuity and knowledge retention
- Scalability — you can transition to full-time as the business demands it
